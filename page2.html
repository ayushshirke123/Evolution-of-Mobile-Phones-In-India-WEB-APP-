Flip Phones Era (1996-2005)

← Back

Motorola StarTAC (1996)

The Motorola StarTAC was one of the first widely adopted flip phones, revolutionizing the mobile industry with its compact design.

It introduced vibration alerts, external displays, and a truly portable form factor, making it a cultural icon of the late 1990s.

Nokia 7110 (1999)

The Nokia 7110 was the first phone with a WAP browser, bringing basic mobile internet to users.

It featured a unique spring-loaded sliding cover for the keypad, popularized in the movie *The Matrix*.

Sanyo SCP-5300 (2002)

The Sanyo SCP-5300 was one of the first flip phones with a built-in camera, pioneering mobile photography.

It had a 640x480 pixel camera, a color display, and a flash, making it a premium option for early adopters.

Nokia 7200 (2003)

The Nokia 7200 was Nokia's first clamshell phone, featuring a stylish fabric cover design.

It had a VGA camera, an external monochrome display, and a strong battery life, appealing to fashion-conscious users.

Samsung SGH-X426 (2003)

Samsung’s SGH-X426 was a lightweight flip phone with a bright color display and polyphonic ringtones.

Its sleek design and affordability made it a popular choice in the early 2000s.

Sony Ericsson Z600 (2003)

The Sony Ericsson Z600 was a stylish flip phone with interchangeable covers and Bluetooth connectivity.

It offered a color display, customizable themes, and VGA camera support, making it a premium choice.

Motorola Razr V3 (2004)

The Motorola Razr V3 was one of the most iconic flip phones ever, known for its ultra-thin profile and stylish aluminum body.

It featured a high-resolution internal display, an external screen, and a VGA camera, making it a trendsetter.

Samsung SGH-D500 (2005)

The Samsung SGH-D500 was a breakthrough in mobile design, featuring a sleek slide-up mechanism instead of a traditional flip.

It was Samsung’s first phone with a **1.3 MP camera and LED flash**, along with Bluetooth support and 96 MB of internal storage.
