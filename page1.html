Motorola DynaTAC 8000X

The **Motorola DynaTAC 8000X** was the first commercially available mobile phone, released in **1983** after nearly a decade of development. It was a major breakthrough in mobile communication, paving the way for modern smartphones.

📱 Key Features & Specifications:

  • 🔋 **Battery Life:** 30 minutes of talk time, 10 hours to charge.
  • 📟 **LED Display:** Used for dialing and recalling saved numbers.
  • 📞 **Storage:** Could store up to 30 contact numbers.
  • 📡 **Weight:** 790 grams (1.75 pounds).
  • 📏 **Size:** 25 cm (10 inches) in height.
  • 💰 **Launch Price:** $3,995 in 1984 (~$11,716 in 2023).

📜 Historical Significance:

Before the DynaTAC 8000X, mobile phones were mostly **car phones**, which required large external power sources. The **DynaTAC 8000X was the first truly portable mobile phone**, though it was still quite large by today’s standards.

The development was led by **Martin Cooper**, a Motorola engineer, who made the first-ever mobile phone call on **April 3, 1973** using a prototype of the DynaTAC. He famously called his rival at **Bell Labs**, Dr. Joel Engel, to demonstrate the breakthrough.

📉 Evolution & Successors:

While groundbreaking, the **DynaTAC 8000X** was bulky and expensive. It was later succeeded by the **Motorola MicroTAC (1989)**, which introduced a **flip design**, and the **Motorola StarTAC (1996)**, the first **clamshell (flip) phone**.
